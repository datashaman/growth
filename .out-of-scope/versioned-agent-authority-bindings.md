# Versioned Agent Authority Bindings

Growth does not model versioned, negotiated, revocable autonomy bindings for
agents.

## Why this is out of scope

An authority binding assumes Growth can define and govern an agent's autonomous
decision rights over time. That is beyond the current boundary. Growth serves
advisory context to a Client Agent and records attribution; it does not host,
supervise, or enforce the Client Agent's reasoning loop.

A first-class authority-binding artifact would also depend on a deeper
agent-principal model and an operation-level autonomy policy model. Those would
move Growth from project-context tooling into governance of unattended agent
authority, including approval, expiry, revocation, and enforcement semantics.
That is not part of the current product scope.

Role assignments, Personas, Capabilities, and Capability Surfaces remain useful
inside Growth as advisory context and UI/tool grouping. They should not be
extended into a versioned autonomy contract for independent agents.
